# Ready.gov Wildfire Guidance (public messaging summary)

## Prepare before a wildfire

- Maintain multiple alert pathways (community alerts, EAS/WEA, FEMA app / NWS alerts).
- Make a household emergency plan, including workplaces and schools.
- Know evacuation zones/routes; practice with pets; pre-identify destinations.
- Create defensible space concepts (Ready.gov highlights a fire-resistant zone free of debris/flammables for at least ~30 feet from the home as a preparedness practice).
- Monitor air quality alerts.

## During a wildfire

- Evacuate immediately if authorities order it.
- Follow official routes; do not rely on shortcuts that may be blocked.
- Find shelter information via local officials, FEMA app, or Red Cross emergency apps.
- If trapped, call 9-1-1, give location, and turn on lights to aid rescuers (response may be delayed).
- If not ordered to evacuate but smoke is heavy, stay inside or move to a cleaner-air community location; consider N95 protection when appropriate.

## Evacuation planning reminders (Ready.gov evacuation pages)

- Decide destination in advance (family/friends or designated shelter).
- Learn state/local evacuation zone and route plans.
- Take the emergency supply kit; notify out-of-state contacts.
- Unplug small electronics as advised; shut off utilities only if instructed and safe.

## Relation to RapidResponseAgent

Public preparedness messaging is **life-safety and protective-action** focused. Post-event building-damage maps support recovery briefings and re-entry prioritization after the fire front and evacuations are managed by authorities.
