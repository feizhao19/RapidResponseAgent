# NIMS / ICS Basics (FEMA summary)

## What NIMS is

The National Incident Management System (NIMS) is a nationwide framework so government, NGOs, and the private sector can work together to prevent, protect against, mitigate, respond to, and recover from incidents of any size or complexity.

NIMS provides shared vocabulary, systems, and processes that support the National Preparedness System.

## Incident Command System (ICS)

ICS is a standardized on-scene approach to command, control, and coordination. It provides a common hierarchy so personnel from multiple organizations can work together effectively.

ICS applies across disciplines and includes five major functional areas, staffed as needed:

1. **Command**
2. **Operations**
3. **Planning**
4. **Logistics**
5. **Finance/Administration**

Incident command is responsible for overall incident management (single Incident Commander or Unified Command).

## Command and coordination structures

NIMS command and coordination commonly includes:

- **ICS** (on-scene management)
- **Emergency Operations Centers (EOCs)** (off-site multiagency support and resource coordination)
- **MAC Group** (high-level policy / scarce-resource allocation support)
- **Joint Information System (JIS)** (coordinated public messaging)

Together these form Multiagency Coordination Systems (MACS) concepts used in complex incidents.

## Implications for RapidResponseAgent

- Damage maps and AOI statistics are **Planning / Situational Awareness** products—they inform briefings but do not replace ICS command authority or resource ordering.
- Hospital proximity and weather briefs can feed Operations and Planning discussions; keep them labeled as assessment intelligence.
- Use NIMS terms (ICS, EOC, Unified Command, JIS) when generating EOC-facing language so outputs align with partner expectations.
