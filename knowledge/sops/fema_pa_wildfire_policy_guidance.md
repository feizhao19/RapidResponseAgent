# FEMA Public Assistance — Wildfire Policy Guidance

## Why wildfire PA needs special attention

Wildfire incidents can differ from other disasters because of:

- longer incident periods;
- mixed natural, technological, and human causes of damage;
- complex debris and demolition needs across public and private property.

FEMA issued wildfire-specific Public Assistance (PA) guidance to better align eligibility determinations with these conditions. Except where the memo states otherwise, assistance remains subject to the Public Assistance Program and Policy Guide (PAPPG) Version 4.

## Private Property Debris Removal (PPDR)

Key points from the FEMA wildfire PA guidance memo:

- Pre-approval from FEMA is **not required** for an Applicant to begin PPDR work (including privately owned residential and commercial property).
- To receive PA funding, FEMA must still determine that the work is eligible.
- The Applicant must notify FEMA that PPDR is being conducted and the property type (for example residential vs commercial).
- The Applicant must submit written documentation identifying the specific properties or areas where PPDR occurred.
- Documentation must show legal authority/responsibility to perform PPDR and compliance with applicable law, ordinance, and code.

## Debris, demolition, and recovery activities

For federally declared incidents, FEMA uses PAPPG Version 4 together with the wildfire memo when making eligibility determinations for:

- debris removal;
- demolition;
- related response and recovery activities tied to wildfire damage.

## Analyst / EOC briefing notes

- Imagery-based damage assessments can help prioritize inspection and debris corridors, but **PA eligibility** still depends on FEMA policy and Applicant documentation.
- Keep PPDR notification and property documentation in the incident file early; do not wait until reimbursement packing.
- Treat this summary as orientation only; cite the official memo and PAPPG for funding decisions.
