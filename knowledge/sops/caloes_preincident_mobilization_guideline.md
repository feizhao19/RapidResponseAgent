# Cal OES Pre-Incident Mobilization Preparedness Guideline (summary)

## Purpose

This guideline addresses **resource augmentation before** anticipated significant fire or other disaster events. It helps Operational Areas (OA) and mutual aid regions determine augmentation levels for personnel, equipment, and crews.

It is a preparedness tool, not a substitute for incident-specific ICS orders once an incident is underway.

## Resource request process (high level)

1. Local Fire Chief and/or OA Coordinator identify a need to preposition resources (OES equipment preferred when available).
2. Cal OES approves a request period (often 12 hours; sometimes 24 hours as conditions warrant).
3. OA Coordinator provides the Preparedness/Mobilization Resource Approval Form to OA dispatch and the Cal OES Fire and Rescue Regional Coordinator.
4. Dispatch creates/fills requests using Preparedness/Preposition as the incident type.
5. Cal OES control numbers are documented in resource-order references.
6. Unfilled needs escalate from OA to regional to state channels as needed.
7. At period end, re-evaluate; demobilize when preposition resources are no longer required.

## Analyst note

Prepositioning is about **forecast threat and readiness posture**. Satellite/aerial damage products become more central after ignition for impact mapping, re-entry, and recovery documentation—not for authorizing preposition grants themselves.
