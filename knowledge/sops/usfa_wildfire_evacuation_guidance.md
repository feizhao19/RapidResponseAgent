# National Wildfire Evacuation Planning Guidance (USFA/FEMA summary)

## Purpose

This guidance provides an adaptable framework for federal, state, local, tribal, and territorial agencies to develop and implement wildfire evacuation plans. It includes a Wildfire Evacuation Plan Template as a starting point.

Primary goal: **save lives**.

## Scope and disclaimer

Wildfire prevention and management is multi-agency. The document compiles guidelines and best practices for evacuation planning. It is **not** a set of absolute requirements. Local context, resources, and changing crisis conditions must always be considered.

## Why WUI matters

Wildfires are especially dangerous in the wildland–urban interface (WUI), where development meets or mixes with vegetation, and in urban settings where buildings abut islands of green space. Fast-moving fires can create sudden life-safety threats that demand pre-planned evacuation triggers and routes.

## Planning themes for EOC use

Effective wildfire evacuation planning typically addresses:

- clear decision triggers and authority to order evacuations;
- public warning and notification pathways;
- primary / alternate routes and transportation support;
- sheltering and re-entry considerations;
- coordination across fire, law enforcement, emergency management, and public information;
- exercises and after-action improvement.

## Link to imagery assessment products

Building-damage maps and AOI statistics help recovery and public information after the fire front passes. Evacuation decisions during active spread rely more on fire behavior, weather, and life-safety command intent than on post-event damage tallies.

Use assessment products to:

- brief impacts for re-entry planning;
- prioritize inspections along evacuated corridors;
- support public messaging with verified structure-loss figures after validation.
