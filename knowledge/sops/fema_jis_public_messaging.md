# Joint Information System (JIS) basics (summary)

## Role

Within NIMS command and coordination, the **Joint Information System (JIS)** helps ensure coordinated, accurate public messaging among ICS, EOCs, and MAC Groups.

Public Information processes communicate timely, accurate, accessible information to the public, media, and partners.

## Practical rules for analysts generating chat/report language

- Separate **verified assessment facts** (building counts, damage classes, citations) from **protective-action guidance** (evacuate now, shelter locations).
- Prefer official Ready.gov / local EO messaging for life-safety instructions.
- When sharing RapidResponseAgent maps or stats externally, label them as preliminary remote-sensing assessment products pending field validation.
- Route media inquiries through the jurisdiction’s Public Information Officer / JIS process rather than ad-hoc AI wording.

## Tie-in to product features

- Assessment reports and damage stats can feed talking points once validated.
- Weather/red-flag and evacuation knowledge docs support consistent risk language.
- Hospital distance answers should avoid implying facility operational status without verification.
