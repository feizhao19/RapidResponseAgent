# Community Lifelines / Critical Facilities (summary)

## Concept

FEMA’s Community Lifelines construct helps incident managers prioritize stabilization of the services communities need for response and recovery. Lifelines organize consequences and interdependencies (for example safety/security, food/water/shelter, health/medical, energy, communications, transportation, hazardous materials).

Exact lifeline taxonomy and tools evolve; use current FEMA lifelines materials for formal doctrine.

## Why hospitals and utilities matter in AOI briefs

During and after wildfire or other disasters, EOC questions often include:

- nearest hospitals / trauma capacity context;
- whether critical facilities lost power, access, or staffing;
- whether debris or fire damage blocks lifeline corridors.

PDA Pocket Guide style IDA collection also asks about widespread loss of critical facilities (hospitals, grocery stores) and essential utilities, plus shortages of food, water, and medical supplies.
