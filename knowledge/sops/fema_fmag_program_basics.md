# FMAG Program Basics (FEMA P-954 summary)

## Purpose

Under the Fire Management Assistance Grant (FMAG) Program, FEMA provides financial assistance (grants) to State, Tribal, and local governments for the mitigation, management, and control of declared fires on public or private forest land or grassland that threaten destruction comparable to a major disaster.

Eligible cost categories commonly discussed in program materials include equipment, supplies, and personnel costs associated with declared fire response.

## Who uses the guide

The FMAG guide is intended for:

- elected leaders;
- emergency managers;
- public works directors;
- financial management personnel;
- other officials responsible for wildfire response and reimbursement documentation.

## Program context

FMAG replaced FEMA’s earlier Fire Suppression Assistance Program after Stafford Act amendments (Disaster Mitigation Act of 2000) and applies to fires declared on or after October 30, 2001 (per the guide).

Shared understanding of policies and procedures across partners is essential for correct implementation and reimbursement.

## Practical implications for Rapid Response workflows

- Remote-sensing damage products support situational awareness and prioritization; they do **not** by themselves prove FMAG cost eligibility.
- Pair geospatial damage summaries with operational logs (resources ordered, burn period, threat narrative) when preparing declaration / grant support packages.
- Confirm current cost thresholds and documentation checklists with the latest FEMA FMAG materials for the incident year.
