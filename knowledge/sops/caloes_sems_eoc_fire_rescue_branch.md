# Operational Area EOC Operations — Fire & Rescue Branch (summary)

## Role of the Fire & Rescue Branch

In an Operational Area EOC Operations Section, the Fire and Rescue Branch links the EOC to:

- fire department operations/dispatch centers;
- the fire and rescue mutual aid system;
- incident command for incidents managed by fire services (as appropriate).

The Branch Coordinator reports to the Operations Section Coordinator.

## Typical responsibilities

- Coordinate fire, hazardous materials, and urban search and rescue operations in the jurisdictional area.
- Assist the Fire & Rescue Mutual Aid System Coordinator in acquiring mutual aid resources.
- Coordinate mobilization/transportation of resources through Logistics.
- Obtain situation reports and resource-support requests (outside mutual aid, when applicable).
- Push EOC-developed information and directives to local EOCs, fire DOCs, and ICs as appropriate.

Smaller jurisdictions may combine fire operations, fire & rescue, and hazmat functions.

## How imagery assessments help EOC Operations

- Provide common operating-picture layers for burned/impacted neighborhoods.
- Support prioritization of life-safety follow-up, utility restoration corridors, and inspection teams.
- Keep products labeled as **assessment intelligence**, distinct from resource-ordering authority held by Fire & Rescue / Logistics channels.
