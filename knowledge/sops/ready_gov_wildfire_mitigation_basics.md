# Wildfire impact mitigation and protective actions

## Purpose

Orient analysts and EOC staff on **public, non-legal** actions that reduce wildfire impacts on people and property. This is not a substitute for local evacuation orders or official hazard mitigation plans.

## Immediate protective actions (response window)

- Follow official evacuation / shelter-in-place orders; do not wait for personal confirmation beyond trusted official channels.
- Move people and critical records first; vehicles and pets next when time allows.
- Shut off gas/propane only if trained and instructed by officials; never re-enter a burned structure without clearance.
- Use N95/P100 respiratory protection when ash and smoke are present outdoors.
- Check on medically vulnerable neighbors when it is safe and authorized to do so.

## Short-term mitigation after the fire front

- Document damage with photos/GPS before cleanup when safe (supports PDA / insurance / assistance pathways).
- Avoid ash, debris, and unstable structures; treat burned debris as potentially hazardous.
- Restore power, water, and access routes only through coordinated lifeline / public works channels.
- Prioritize hospitals, EOCs, water systems, and evacuation routes when sequencing repairs (community lifelines).

## Longer-term mitigation (community / property)

- Create and maintain defensible space and ember-resistant vents where local codes allow.
- Harden roofs/siding and clear gutters of fuels.
- Update household and facility emergency plans; pre-identify shelter and mutual-aid needs.
- Participate in local hazard mitigation planning and FMAG / Public Assistance processes when declared.

## Relation to RapidResponseAgent products

- Damage maps and building counts describe **impact**, not mitigation completeness.
- Use imagery products to prioritize **where** protective actions and inspections are most urgent (e.g., destroyed / major clusters).
- For funding and declaration questions, use FMAG / PA / declarations guidance; for messaging, use JIS basics.
- Always cite the official Ready.gov / local OEM source before advising the public.

## Limits

This summary does not create eligibility for grants, change evacuation law, or replace Cal OES / FEMA operational orders.
