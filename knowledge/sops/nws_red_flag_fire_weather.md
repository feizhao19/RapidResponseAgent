# NWS Fire Weather Watch / Red Flag Warning (summary)

## Purpose

The National Weather Service issues **Fire Weather Watches** and **Red Flag Warnings** to alert the public and land-management / fire agencies when weather conditions, combined with dry fuels, can support dangerous wildfire ignition or extreme fire behavior.

## Product meanings

- **Red Flag Warning:** Critical fire weather conditions are occurring or expected soon (often within about 24–48 hours, depending on office practice). Signals elevated potential for rapid spread and difficult control.
- **Fire Weather Watch:** High potential that Red Flag criteria may develop later (commonly on the order of 12–96 hours ahead, office-dependent). Listen for updates and possible upgrade to a warning.

Exact timing windows and meteorological thresholds **vary by NWS Weather Forecast Office** because vegetation, topography, and climate differ regionally.

## Typical ingredients (conceptual)

Local criteria often combine some of:

- low relative humidity;
- strong or gusty winds;
- dry/receptive fuels (confirmed with fire-management partners);
- sometimes dry thunderstorm / lightning risk.

Always check the local NWS office criteria and the specific Watch/Warning text for the AOI.

## Operational use with RapidResponseAgent weather tools

- When the chat weather brief shows red-flag / fire-weather context, treat it as **life-safety and fire-behavior risk**, not as a building-damage statistic.
- Pair red-flag context with evacuation/public messaging guidance (Ready.gov / USFA) and with post-event damage maps only after the active-spread phase is managed by authorities.
- Do not invent numeric RH/wind thresholds for a county unless retrieved from a local NWS definition or live forecast product.
