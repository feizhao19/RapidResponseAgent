# FEMA Preliminary Damage Assessment Guide (summary)

## Purpose

The FEMA Preliminary Damage Assessment Guide defines a national framework for how state, local, tribal, and territorial (SLTT) officials, FEMA staff, and partners collect, validate, quantify, and document the cause, location, and details of damage after a disaster.

PDAs help align damage documentation with Stafford Act Presidential disaster declaration request needs and support timely delivery of recovery assistance.

## Key definitions

- **Initial Damage Assessment (IDA):** Local/tribal effort to collect damage extent data within a jurisdiction.
- **Joint Preliminary Damage Assessment (joint PDA):** Coordinated SLTT + federal validation of IDA information to inform declaration requests and federal grant determinations.
- **Preliminary Damage Assessment (PDA):** Broader mechanism to determine impact/magnitude of damage and resulting unmet needs.

## Standard PDA process (baseline)

1. Local government or Tribal Nation conducts the **IDA**.
2. State/Tribal/territorial (STT) government reviews damage.
3. STT government requests a **joint PDA**.
4. STT government and FEMA conduct the joint PDA.
5. Using joint PDA results, the STT government may submit a Presidential disaster declaration request.

Steps may be compressed, concurrent, or expedited depending on the incident. Expedited declarations may proceed with limited or deferred joint PDA when severity/magnitude clearly exceeds STT capacity.

## Methods relevant to remote sensing products

Chapter 3 methodologies include self-reporting, door-to-door assessments, digital surveys, windshield surveys, virtual sensing, GIS, modeling, and desktop assessments.

FEMA notes that disasters such as fires, tornadoes, and hurricanes often produce readily visible damage that can be assessed quickly via windshield surveys, flyovers, or **geospatial analysis**, while floods/earthquakes may need more door-to-door work.

IDA documentation commonly includes geotagged photos, GIS products, and drone/agency imagery shared with regional PDA teams for validation.

## Individual Assistance vs Public Assistance PDAs

- **IA PDAs** focus on residential impacts and survivor unmet needs (see IA damage levels and matrices in the full guide).
- **PA PDAs** focus on public infrastructure, debris, emergency protective measures, and facilities (see PA documentation/work eligibility matrices).

Other federal pathways (for example Mitigation, FMAG, SBA loans) may also influence what information is collected.

## Implications for RapidResponseAgent users

- Building-damage maps and AOI statistics can support IDA/joint PDA **situational awareness** and prioritization.
- Geospatial outputs should be paired with geotagged field validation, accessibility notes, and critical-facility impacts.
- PDA products inform declaration requests; they are not themselves a grant eligibility determination.
- Always cite the official PDA Guide / Pocket Guide for operational PDA work.
