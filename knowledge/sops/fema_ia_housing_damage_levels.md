# FEMA IA Housing Damage Levels (summary)

## Why this matters

For Individual Assistance (IA) preliminary damage assessments, FEMA uses standardized **degrees of damage** for homes so teams can quickly categorize impacts for declaration support. These categories are for FEMA IA/PDA reporting and are **not identical** to every local building-tagging scheme (for example red/yellow tags) or to a remote-sensing model’s class labels.

## Four degrees of damage (high level)

FEMA commonly uses:

- **Affected:** mostly cosmetic damage.
- **Minor:** repairable non-structural damage.
- **Major:** structural damage or other significant damage needing extensive repairs.
- **Destroyed:** total loss / not reasonably repairable as a dwelling.

Pocket-guide practice emphasizes efficiency: in large events, teams may not perform a detailed engineering review of every home.

## Essential living spaces

Assessments consider essential living spaces (bedroom, bathroom, kitchen, living room regularly used by household members) when judging habitability-related impacts.

## Flood-oriented examples (conventionally built homes)

Public IA summaries often illustrate flood waterlines (examples used in FEMA materials):

- Minor: waterline at or below about 18 inches in essential living spaces (conventionally built), among other non-structural criteria.
- Major: waterline above about 18 inches or reaching electrical outlets / more severe structural issues.

Always use the current PDA Guide matrices for the incident period; thresholds and tables are updated over time.

## Mapping to RapidResponseAgent damage classes

RapidResponseAgent/ViPDE chat classes are typically: **No damage / Minor / Major / Destroyed**.

Use this FEMA IA summary to:

- explain how federal PDA teams talk about housing damage;
- caution that model outputs are **assessment intelligence**, while FEMA IA degrees are assigned through PDA field/joint processes;
- avoid claiming a pixel/building model label automatically equals a FEMA IA eligibility determination.
