# Cal OES Fire & Rescue Mutual Aid Plan (summary)

## Purpose

The California Fire Service and Rescue Emergency Mutual Aid Plan supports systematic mobilization, organization, and operation of fire and rescue resources across the state and its political subdivisions for disasters (natural or human-caused).

It extends and supports the California Emergency Plan and is intended to align with ICS, integrated emergency management concepts, and multi-hazard planning. More detailed local/area/regional operational plans should sit under this framework.

## Planning levels

California fire and rescue emergency operations planning occurs at:

1. Local
2. Operational Area
3. Regional
4. State

Plans at each level should be developed within the Mutual Aid Plan framework so resources can be requested and filled consistently.

## Core concept

Mobilization planning aims to provide a local fire/rescue authority sufficient resources **without extraordinarily depleting** fire and rescue defenses outside the disaster area.

## Relevance to Rapid Response imagery workflows

- Mutual aid decisions are command/dispatch driven; damage maps help brief impact geography and priority corridors after/during incidents.
- Do not treat AOI damage statistics as a mutual-aid ordering authority—use them as supporting situational awareness for EOC/Fire & Rescue coordination.
