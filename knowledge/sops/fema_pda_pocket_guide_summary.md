# FEMA PDA Pocket Guide (summary)

## What PDAs are for

The Preliminary Damage Assessment process estimates the magnitude of damage and unmet needs for individuals, communities, businesses, and the public sector. PDA data helps STT governments decide whether to request a Presidential declaration and helps FEMA form a recommendation.

Use the Pocket Guide as a field quick reference; use the full PDA Guide for detailed procedures.

## Information SLTT partners should collect early (IDA / self-reporting)

FEMA encourages self-reporting with:

- geotagged photos;
- GIS products;
- drone imagery when available.

Useful data categories include:

- private (non-agricultural) and public property damage;
- debris and emergency protective activities;
- private nonprofit and public facility impacts;
- inaccessible areas, health/safety hazards, emergency protective measure needs;
- widespread loss of critical facilities (for example hospitals, grocery stores) and essential utilities;
- shortages of food, water, and medical supplies.

## Analyst briefing tip

Remote-sensing damage layers are most useful when they can be cross-walked to these PDA evidence packages (location, photo/imagery, facility/utility impacts, access constraints).
